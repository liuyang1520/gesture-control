## Overview

Gesture Control processes camera input on-device to detect hand and eye movement for pointer control features on macOS.

## Data Collection

Gesture Control does not collect, transmit, sell, or share personal data with external services.

## Camera Access

Gesture Control requests camera access to analyze live video frames for gesture and eye tracking.

- Camera frames are processed in memory on the user’s Mac.
- Camera frames are not uploaded to external servers.
- Camera frames are not retained after processing unless the user independently records them outside the app.

## Accessibility Access

Gesture Control requests Accessibility permission on macOS so it can move the pointer, click, scroll, and send navigation shortcuts on the user’s Mac.

- Accessibility permission is used only to perform the input actions requested by the user through the app’s controls.
- Accessibility data is not transmitted to external services.

## Data Storage

Gesture Control may store local app preferences such as:

- Selected camera
- Tracking settings
- Eye calibration data

These settings remain on the user’s device unless they choose to back up or sync their Mac using Apple services outside the app.

## Children’s Privacy

Gesture Control is not directed to children under 13, and the app does not knowingly collect personal information from children.
